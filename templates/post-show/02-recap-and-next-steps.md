# Recap and Next Steps

> **When to use:** You had a substantive conversation and want to summarize key points and propose next steps.
> **Best timing:** 1-3 days after the event
> **Expected response rate:** 25-35%

## Subject Line Options

1. `[Show Name] recap — next steps for [Company Name]`
2. `Following up on our [Show Name] discussion`
3. `[First Name], here's what we discussed + next steps`

## Email Body

Hi [First Name],

Thanks again for the conversation at [Show Name]. I wanted to send a quick recap and suggest some next steps.

**What we discussed:**
- [Point 1 — their challenge or goal]
- [Point 2 — how your solution relates]
- [Point 3 — any specific requests or questions they had]

**Suggested next steps:**
1. [Next step 1 — e.g., I'll send over the case study we mentioned]
2. [Next step 2 — e.g., Schedule a 30-min demo for your team]
3. [Next step 3 — e.g., Introduce you to our [Role] who specializes in this]

Would [Date Option 1] or [Date Option 2] work for a follow-up call? I can send a calendar invite.

Looking forward to it.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Title], [Your Company]

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| [First Name] | Their first name | Robert |
| [Show Name] | Trade show name | IMTS 2026 |
| [Company Name] | Their company | PrecisionWorks |
| [Point 1-3] | Discussion topics | Reducing setup time for CNC operations |
| [Next step 1-3] | Proposed actions | Send ROI calculator spreadsheet |
| [Date Option 1-2] | Proposed call dates | Thursday 3/12 or Friday 3/13 |
| [Your Name] | Your full name | Alex Chen |

## Tips

- Recap emails convert at higher rates because they demonstrate you listened
- Keep the recap bullets factual — don't oversell what was discussed
- Propose 2 specific dates instead of saying "let me know when you're free"
