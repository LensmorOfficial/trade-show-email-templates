# Long-Term Nurture

> **When to use:** The prospect isn't ready to buy now, but you want to stay on their radar for when timing is right.
> **Best timing:** Monthly or quarterly touchpoints
> **Expected response rate:** 5-10% per touch (compounds over time)

## Subject Line Options

1. `[First Name], thought of you when I saw this`
2. `Quick share — [Topic] trends`
3. `[Industry] update you might find interesting`

## Email Body

### Version A — Share an insight

Hi [First Name],

Hope things have been going well since [Show Name].

I came across [Content/Article/Data] about [Topic] and thought of our conversation. Here it is: [URL]

The part about [specific takeaway] seems especially relevant to what [Company Name] is working on.

No ask — just wanted to share. Let me know if you'd ever like to pick up where we left off.

Best,
[Your Name]

### Version B — Upcoming event trigger

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company Name] is exhibiting at [Next Show Name] in [Month]. We'll be there too.

It's been a while since we connected at [Original Show Name]. Would be great to catch up in person at [Next Show Name] if you're open to it.

Let me know — happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[Your Name]

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| [First Name] | Their first name | Sandra |
| [Show Name] | Original show where you met | SIAL Paris |
| [Next Show Name] | Upcoming event | Anuga 2027 |
| [Company Name] | Their company | Global Foods Ltd |
| [Topic] | Relevant industry topic | sustainable packaging |
| [URL] | Link to content | https://example.com/trends |
| [Your Name] | Your full name | Alex Chen |

## Tips

- Nurture emails should give value with zero pressure to buy
- Space them 4-8 weeks apart — enough to stay remembered without being annoying
- Track when their company exhibits at new shows — it's the perfect reason to re-engage
- After 3-4 nurture touches, some will convert naturally when their timing is right
