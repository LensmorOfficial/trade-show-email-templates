# Initial Introduction

> **When to use:** You've identified a company exhibiting at an upcoming trade show and want to introduce yourself before the event.
> **Best timing:** 4-6 weeks before the show
> **Expected response rate:** 15-25%

## Subject Line Options

1. `Attending [Show Name]? Let's connect`
2. `[Show Name] — quick intro from [Your Company]`
3. `Saw you're exhibiting at [Show Name]`

## Email Body

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company Name] is exhibiting at [Show Name] in [City] this [Month]. We'll be there too.

[Your Company] works with [Industry] companies on [brief value prop — one sentence]. I think there could be some interesting overlap with what you're doing at [Company Name].

Would you be open to a quick chat at the show? Happy to stop by your booth or meet wherever is convenient.

Either way, looking forward to the event.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Title], [Your Company]

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| [First Name] | Recipient's first name | Sarah |
| [Company Name] | Their company | Acme Corp |
| [Show Name] | Trade show name | MEDICA 2026 |
| [City] | Event city | Dusseldorf |
| [Month] | Event month | November |
| [Your Company] | Your company name | Lensmor |
| [Industry] | Their industry | medical device |
| [Your Name] | Your full name | Alex Chen |
| [Your Title] | Your job title | Head of Partnerships |

## Tips

- Keep the first email short — your goal is just to open the conversation, not close a deal
- Mention something specific about their company to show you did your research
- Don't attach anything — links and attachments lower deliverability on first touch
