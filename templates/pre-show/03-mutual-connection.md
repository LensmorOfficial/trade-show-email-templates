# Mutual Connection Introduction

> **When to use:** You share a mutual contact who can serve as a warm introduction.
> **Best timing:** 3-5 weeks before the show
> **Expected response rate:** 30-40%

## Subject Line Options

1. `[Mutual Contact] suggested we connect at [Show Name]`
2. `Intro via [Mutual Contact] — see you at [Show Name]?`
3. `[Mutual Contact] thought we should meet`

## Email Body

Hi [First Name],

[Mutual Contact Name] mentioned that you'll be at [Show Name] and suggested we connect. I lead [Your Role] at [Your Company] — we focus on [brief value prop].

[Mutual Contact Name] thought there might be a good fit given your work in [their specific area]. I'd love to grab coffee or meet at your booth during the event.

Would [Day] work for a quick chat?

Best,
[Your Name]
[Your Title], [Your Company]

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| [First Name] | Recipient's first name | Lisa |
| [Mutual Contact Name] | Shared connection | David Park |
| [Show Name] | Trade show name | Hannover Messe |
| [Your Role] | Your role description | business development |
| [Your Company] | Your company | Lensmor |
| [Day] | Proposed day | Wednesday afternoon |
| [Your Name] | Your full name | Alex Chen |
| [Your Title] | Your job title | BD Manager |

## Tips

- Always get permission from the mutual contact before name-dropping
- The mutual connection is your credibility — lead with it in the subject line
- This template has the highest response rate of all pre-show emails because trust is pre-built
