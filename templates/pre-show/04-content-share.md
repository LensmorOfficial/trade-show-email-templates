# Content Share

> **When to use:** You want to provide value first by sharing useful content, then transition to a meeting request.
> **Best timing:** 5-6 weeks before the show (gives time for a follow-up meeting request)
> **Expected response rate:** 10-20%

## Subject Line Options

1. `[Industry] report you might find useful — plus [Show Name]`
2. `Thought of you: [Content Title]`
3. `Quick share before [Show Name]`

## Email Body

Hi [First Name],

I came across our latest [report/guide/analysis] on [Content Topic] and thought it might be relevant given [Company Name]'s focus on [their area].

Here's the link: [Content URL]

The key takeaway: [one-sentence summary of the insight].

By the way, we'll both be at [Show Name] in [Month]. If the report resonates, I'd love to discuss it briefly at the event — no pitch, just a conversation about what we're seeing in the market.

Best,
[Your Name]

## Variables

| Variable | Description | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| [First Name] | Recipient's first name | James |
| [Company Name] | Their company | Nordic Sensors |
| [Content Topic] | What the content is about | exhibitor lead conversion benchmarks |
| [Content URL] | Link to the content | https://www.lensmor.com/blog/report |
| [Show Name] | Trade show name | Electronica 2026 |
| [Month] | Event month | November |
| [Your Name] | Your full name | Alex Chen |

## Tips

- The content must be genuinely useful — not a product brochure disguised as a report
- Industry data, benchmarks, and trend reports work best
- This is a two-step approach: content first, meeting request in a follow-up email 1-2 weeks later
